Midpoint Report

Midpoint Insights:

  As a team, we feel our idea has a solid base with good intentions of helping the freshman students at UF. Following the UCD process gave us confidence in our idea and that it would benefit users in the way we were intending. Peers though that we had a desirable idea and product which freshman (and themselves) would use. Our prototype had some criticism as some felt it didn’t go in depth on the features of our app, but the response was that our application had potential and room for growth.

Colleague Suggestions:

    It came to our attention that the application wouldn’t be able to compete with google maps and doesn’t have a longer use case since once students know where their classes are as they will simple stop using the product. To address the first point, this product isn’t meant to compete with google maps, it is an edge case which we are creating an application to fill. Google maps does provide the location of a class’s building, however there are a few flaws which out application addresses: the bus route system and the location of the classroom within the building. The UF bus routes are not properly displayed on google maps, it has bus times listed to arrive around 10–15 minutes every stop which isn’t the case. Our application would work with the current RTS Tracking App system to give proper times for bus arrivals and departures to give students a more accurate times for them to get to their classes. Our other main feature will show the location of the classroom within the building. We have received a criticism that most people will be able to find their classroom once the building can be found, which is true. However, our focus is convenience, students will not have to wander around the halls to find the classroom or figure out the numbering convention when it comes to where the rooms are laid out in the halls. In addition, there are some buildings which have layouts that are not easy to navigate, the CISE building is one of them with two floors of rooms having sequential numbering PLUS classroom that are not accessible without entering another room first. To a first time student, this can be confusing. To address the second point, our application is mean to have this specific use case. Once students know where their classes are then they may not have use for the application however there will always be more freshman to UF AND there will always be more semesters for students where they will need to know where their classes are. There is a steady supply of users, and our application will have peaks in its use. This design was intentional and meant to be an educational tool, in a sense, to help students get used to their campus. This application will cut down on the time it takes to search for classrooms and allow students experience less stress when navigating the expansive UF campus.

Our Plans for the Future

Exploration:

 Our plan from here on is to develop our main features even further than we have currently. Through the development of our prototype, we can further demonstrate our mapping application and test them through the use of created personas and through user testing to explore the needs of the user and tune our application to them.

Ideation:

 Our focus will be on prototyping and testing the features we deemed to be essential to the functionality of our application. Our high-fidelity prototype will be based on the features showcased on our poster and will be created using InVision. We will accompany the prototype with a demonstrational video which can be posted to this website to showcase the progress towards the final product of our application.

Refinement:

 We plan to demonstrate our prototype through user-based testing as well as creating personas to help us develop and understand the use cases of our idea. Through the feedback we receive, we plan to tune our application to provide the essential functionality combined with accessibility to give the best experience to our users.
